Project 01
Residential / 460 m²

Maison de Lumière

A townhouse renovation organized around daylight, slower circulation, and material continuity. The intervention opens the rear façade, reshapes the stair as a spatial anchor, and creates a calmer threshold between street-facing rooms and the garden sequence.

LocationGhent, Belgium
StatusCompleted in 2025
MaterialityOak, lime plaster, stone
01
Concept framing

Redefined the plan around light penetration, a stronger rear connection, and a more legible circulation spine.

02
Plan & section studies

Tested openings, privacy gradients, and stair geometry through iterative plan and sectional analysis.

03
Technical development

Resolved joinery, structure, and finish transitions into a warm but highly controlled architectural language.

Description

The project prioritizes atmosphere without decorative excess. The result feels warm, quiet, and precise.

Plans

The media carousel includes a ground-floor plan, circulation diagram, interior photographs, and detail studies.

Living volume with sculpted daylight and a restrained material palette.

Project 02
Mixed use / 820 m²

Atelier Courtyard

A former industrial structure transformed into a studio-residence hybrid. Instead of filling the shell, the design removes mass to insert a courtyard that organizes circulation, light, and a sense of calm across studio, exhibition, and domestic functions.

LocationAntwerp, Belgium
StatusIn progress
ProgramStudio, gallery, residence
01
Site reading

Mapped inherited structure, service zones, and opportunities for adaptive reuse without losing character.

02
Courtyard insertion

Used subtraction to generate daylight, orientation, and a more legible internal sequence.

03
Atmosphere calibration

Balanced retained industrial texture with quieter domestic surfaces and gallery-grade lighting.

Description

The project is driven by contrast: robust inherited mass outside, carefully calibrated calm inside.

Plans

The media system combines diagrams, plans, interior references, and hero views in a single narrative rail.

Adaptive reuse concept balancing industrial shell and a calm courtyard sequence.

Project 03
Coastal home / Concept

Cliff House

A coastal house shaped by exposure, protection, and horizon views. The architecture uses layered terraces, recessed openings, and mineral finishes to create shelter while framing the sea as the project’s primary interior surface.

LocationKnokke, Belgium
StatusConcept phase
FocusView framing & shelter
01
View mapping

Established the project around horizon axes, privacy protection, and prevailing wind conditions.

02
Sectional massing

Used stepped massing and recessed façades to mediate scale and climate exposure.

03
Material strategy

Paired mineral surfaces with warm timber interiors to keep the architecture tactile but quiet.

Description

This project is about subtraction and framing: what is hidden matters as much as what is revealed.

Plans

The carousel combines renders, sectional studies, and material references into a compact presentation system.

Layered coastal massing shaped around horizon views and protection.

Project 04
Interiors / Completed

Gallery House

An interior architecture project developed around procession, display, and joinery precision. The rooms are simplified into a gallery-like sequence where light, shadow, and carefully edited materials carry most of the expression.

LocationBrussels, Belgium
StatusCompleted
FocusJoinery & display
01
Spatial editing

Reduced visual noise through stricter alignments, concealed storage, and more deliberate wall openings.

02
Joinery development

Designed integrated cabinetry and display surfaces as part of the architecture rather than furniture afterthoughts.

03
Atmosphere tuning

Used warmer tones selectively to keep the overall experience quiet, bright, and highly intentional.

Description

The interior is treated like a sequence of framed moments rather than a set of decorated rooms.

Plans

The carousel includes photography, layout references, joinery logic, and tonal material studies.

Interior architecture built around procession, light, and gallery-like restraint.
